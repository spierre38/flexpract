White Tiger

White tigers are a genetic variation of Bengal tigers, and are not a separate subspecies. .

They are very rare in the wild, as the gene that produces their white fur is a recessive gene that both parents must carry. .

There are estimated to be less than 200 white tigers in the world, with most living in zoos or captivity. .

White tigers are not albino, as they still have some pigmentation in their stripes and eyes.

In the wild, white tigers face numerous challenges, such as being highly visible to predators and prey, and difficulty blending in with their surroundings during hunting. White tigers have been bred in captivity for many years, often to create a popular attraction for zoos and other animal parks. .

Some animal welfare organizations are critical of white tiger breeding programs, as they often involve inbreeding and can result in health problems for the animals.
